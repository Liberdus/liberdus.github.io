  • Transaction and message fees
  • Validator and archiver staking
  • Toll payments (anti-spam and monetization)

LIB exists both as a native coin on the Liberdus mainnet and as a bridged ERC-20 token on networks like Ethereum, Polygon, and BNB Chain to enable liquidity, accessibility, and exchangeability across the wider crypto ecosystem.

ğŸ§© What Are Liquidity Pools?â€‹

A liquidity pool is a smart contract on a decentralized exchange (DEX) that holds two tokens in equal USD value.
When you trade one token for another (e.g., LIB â†’ USDC), the pool automatically adjusts their ratios using an automated market maker (AMM) formula, keeping the market balanced.

Each pool has its own trading pair, such as:

  • LIB / USDC
  • LIB / wETH
  • LIB / MATIC

Liquidity providers (LPs) deposit both tokens into these pools and earn a proportional share of trading fees whenever swaps occur.

Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs) like Uniswap are the foundation of open, permissionless finance.
Unlike centralized exchanges (CEXs), they:

  • Allow anyone to trade directly from their wallet
  • Require no KYC, sign-up, or custody of funds
  • Are governed by smart contracts, not companies
  • Provide transparency, since all trades occur on-chain

For Liberdus, DEXs ensure that users remain in full control of their LIB tokens- in line with the projectâ€™s mission of privacy, decentralization, and user sovereignty.

ğŸ” What Are Trading Pairs?â€‹

A trading pair defines the two assets available for swapping in a liquidity pool.
For example:

  • LIB / USDC lets users trade LIB for the stablecoin USDC
  • LIB / ETH lets users trade LIB for Ethereum
    Each pair represents its own market with independent pricing and liquidity.

The price of LIB is determined dynamically by the ratio of tokens in the pool; meaning every trade slightly changes the balance and therefore the price.
